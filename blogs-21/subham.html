Fold The world around you


Subham Ghosal

This is not today that we come by to see flexible screens for the first time, the first flexible display was made in 1974. Nicholas K. Sheridon, a PARC employee, made it and named it as e-paper (electronic paper) display. The technology evolved in such a way that future breakthroughs will take folding/flexible screens beyond smartphones, tablets, and TVs. This will result in the .“ultimate all screen wearable”

Flexible Displays : Flexible displays are essentially very thin display screens that can be printed onto flexible or stretchable materials and affixed to surfaces or produced in a variety of configurations.

Flexible/Foldable Display Technologies :

  1. Flexible LCDs : Samsung prototyped 5-inch diagonal LCD panel that uses amorphous silicon TFTs. Fujitsu has demonstrated a flexible, 3.8-inch diagonal monochrome and colour cholesteric LCD display. Pixel-isolated LCDs for enhancing a flexible/foldable LCD's mechanical stability.

  2. FOLED, Flexible AMOLED : OLED stands for Organic light emitting device and is made of flexible base material like reflective metal foil, clear plastic film. It is developed by Universal Display Corporation that small-molecule OLEDs could be built on flexible substrates.

    AMOLED is that the abbreviation of Active-matrix organic light-emitting diode. It is an organic light-emitting display device that consists primarily of an OLED, substrate, and a TFT-driven array. AMOLED, with polymer plastic or metal foil as its flexible substrate material, contains a sturdy anti-bending capability and it can realize dynamic bending display or even folding display. It has a lot of application prospects in smart phones, wearable devices, and automotive devices.

  3. E-paper Electrophoretic, Cholesteric LCDs : A display technology designed to imitate thepresence of ordinary ink on paper. EPD or Electrophoretic Paper Display is capable of holding every text and images without consuming electricity. Although it allows the image to be changed later.

    Cholesteric materials are adjusted liquid crystals and immensely suited for reflective, bistable displays. A cholesteric liquid crystal is a sort of liquid crystal with a helical (smooth curve like a spiral) structure. Cholesteric liquid crystals are known as chiral nematic liquid crystals. When current is applied, crystals change from a vertical to a horizontal position. This has a bi-stable nature, requiring no power to maintain an image and very little power to change it.

  4. Electrowetting Displays (EWD), Electrochromic Displays : Developed and patented by Dutch company called Liquavista. This technology merges the benefits of LCDs and e-ink displays, uses a process called electrowetting, which uses small electrical charges to maneuver coloured oil within each pixel. It has all the assets of LCD but with 2x,3x,4x times the performance. Electrowetting is a technique for regulating or actuating small amounts of liquid by changing the liquid's interfacial tension and consequently the macroscopic contact angle, or by producing bulk liquid motion through an interfacial electric stress using an externally supplied electric field.

    A layer of electrochromic material is placed between two electrode layers in an electrochromic display, that changes from one colour to another when stimulated by an electric current. The top electrode layer is formed from transparent plastic. Siemens' electrochromic mixture includes conductive polymers like polyaniline, which allows the screen to work so quickly. The display is controlled by a printed circuit and can be powered by a really thin printable battery or a photovoltaic cell.

  5. Interferometric Modulator Technology : Uses a reflective technology, called interferometric modulation (IMOD). The technology uses microelectromechanical systems (MEMS) technology to mimic the way butterfly wings shimmer - this process is known as biomimetics, or imitating things found in nature.

“Like It Or Not, The Future Is Full Of Foldables”

Although the market is new and many OEMs are yet to step in this world full of Foldable, Rollable, Slidable display screens, but this is going to be the future where it’s essentially like pulling out a drawer extension so you have more space to work with, while maintaining a minimal footprint.
